## Why this exists

S2036 educates readers about their rights — GDPR access, right to be forgotten, right of reply, correction rights. Today readers don't know these exist unless they research jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction.

## Today's surrogate

- Generic `/privacy` policy page mentions GDPR but doesn't operationalize the reader's recourse.

## Page sections (target — FR primary, EN/locale-N siblings)

### 1. Vos droits (overview)
- Right to access your data (Art. 15 GDPR, etc.)
- Right to correction (Art. 16)
- Right to erasure / forgetting (Art. 17)
- Right of reply (Loi 1881 art. 13 in France)
- Right to file a complaint (CNIL FR / ICO UK / etc.)

### 2. How to exercise each right
- Step-by-step with form / email / postal links
- DSAR full bundle request (S1524)
- Ombudsman complaint (S2023)
- Right-of-reply request (per RoR/DMCA doc)

### 3. Our SLA commitments
- Initial ack within 14 days
- Decision within 30-60 days per right
- Escalation to external authorities listed

### 4. Per-jurisdiction sub-pages
- `/vos-droits/france` — CNIL contact, loi 1881
- `/your-rights/canada` — Quebec Law 25, OPC
- `/your-rights/usa` — CCPA, state AGs
- `/ihre-rechte/deutschland` — BfDI, DSGVO
