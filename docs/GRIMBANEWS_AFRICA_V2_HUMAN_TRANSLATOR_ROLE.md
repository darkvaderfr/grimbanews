# GrimbaNews — Africa Edition v2: Human Translator Role

**Status:** plan v0
**Owner:** Lucy Leai (Strategy) + Africa curator (when hired)
**Walks:** Mythos S1753 (translator role) deferred → partial
**Gating dependency:** `GrimbaTranslator` covers automated FR↔EN; human-translation tier for high-stakes Africa pieces is operator-side hiring.

## Why this exists

Automated translation handles 90% of Africa edition needs. The 10% — high-stakes investigations, per-cluster cultural-context pieces, per-locale tone polish — needs human translator.

## Role profile

- Native FR + EN, working knowledge of one of: Arabic, Swahili, Wolof, Lingala, Yoruba, Hausa, Amharic, Portuguese (per region).
- Editorial translation experience (not just literal).
- Africa context expertise (politics, history, cultural).

## Engagement model

- Per-piece contract (€50-200/piece depending on length + complexity).
- Per-quarter pool of 3-5 translators per region.
- Per-translator quality review by Africa curator.

## Workflow

1. Africa curator marks per-piece as "human-translate required."
2. Per-piece routed to translator pool via matching language pair.
3. Per-translation editor review.
4. Per-translation published with human-translator attribution.

## Quality vs cost trade-off

- Auto-translate: free, ~85% quality.
- Human translate: paid, ~98% quality.
- Per-cluster ROI: high-engagement clusters get human; low-engagement stay auto.
